Abstract

Collaborative navigation is an essential ability for a team of robots to reach the destination without collision. To enable collaborative navigation, two key problems should be addressed, including subteaming and adaptive formation control.

In this paper, we represent a team of robots as a graph. Given the graph representation, we propose a novel hierarchical learning framework that integrates a high-level deep graph cut for team splitting, an intermediate-level graph neural network to coordinate robot teammates and low-level control for individual robot navigation, thus enabling collaborative navigation with subteaming and adaptive formation control.

We conduct extensive experiments to evaluate our approach in both simulation and real-world scenarios. Experimental results show that our approach achieves promising results on collaborative navigation with the capabilities of subteaming and adaptive formation control.
